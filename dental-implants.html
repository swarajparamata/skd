Dental Implants FAQ

Most dental implants are made of titanium, a biocompatible material that fuses with the jawbone for long-lasting support.

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia and is generally well tolerated. Most patients report minimal discomfort during recovery.

With proper care and regular checkups, dental implants can last 20 years or more — often a lifetime.

Most healthy adults with sufficient jawbone density are great candidates. A consultation with our team will confirm your eligibility.
